Students describe CS3510 as a math-heavy algorithms course focused on proofs, pseudocode, and conceptual understanding rather than programming. Common topics include dynamic programming, divide and conquer, graph algorithms, and NP-completeness, with some variation by professor.

Extracted source notes:
- One commenter says CS3510 usually covers Dynamic Programming, Divide and Conquer, Graph Problems, and NP-Completeness, and is mostly math proofs and pseudocode rather than actual programming languages.
- A former HTA notes that professors generally use the Dasgupta, Papadimitriou, and Vazirani algorithms textbook, though the order and optional topics vary.
- Common shared topics include divide and conquer, number theory, graph theory such as DFS, shortest paths and MSTs, and NP-completeness.
- Other topics, including Huffman encoding, max flow, FFT, and linear programming, may vary depending on the professor.
- The same commenter says the course is scary to some students because it is more math-heavy than expected, and memorization alone is not enough without understanding.
- Recommended strategies include office hours and practice problems.
