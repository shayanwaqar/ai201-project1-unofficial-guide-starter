Summary:
Students compare CS3220 as more processor-design and implementation-heavy, while CS3210 is described as a deeper operating systems course with C, kernel work, virtual memory, user environments, and time-consuming labs. Some students prefer CS3220 for implementation while others praise CS3210 as rewarding if enough time is available.

Extracted source notes:
- One commenter says CS3220 has students design and implement a processor similar to CS2200, using Verilog on an FPGA and later adding pipelining.
- The same commenter says CS3210 goes much deeper into operating systems than CS2200 and may include industry papers, textbook material, Linux kernel work, and C implementation.
- That commenter personally preferred CS3220 because it was more implementation-heavy and viewed 3210 as too theory-heavy.
- Another commenter says a CS3210 offering modeled after MIT 6.828 was awesome because students build a skeleton OS over the semester with virtual memory, user environments, and later a network driver.
- Multiple comments warn that CS3210 labs require a lot of time per week and stronger knowledge of CPU registers and memory.
- The source supports questions about systems course selection and tradeoffs between OS and processor design.
